How C.L.E.A.R. Works

Repurposing existing NAPS monitoring stations to detect approaching wildfire smoke before it arrives.

Upstream Detection

Monitoring stations 100-600km away detect smoke plumes hours before they reach major cities.

Regression Analysis

20 years of NAPS data used to build correlation models between remote stations and city PM2.5 levels.

Real-time Alerts

Color-coded health warnings triggered automatically when smoke is detected approaching.

From Data to Warning

A four-step process that gives you time to prepare.

1

Data Collection

PM2.5 readings from NAPS and U.S. EPA stations across North America

2

Pattern Analysis

Correlation analysis identifies stations that predict city PM2.5 levels

3

Threshold Check

When upstream stations exceed thresholds, alerts are triggered

4

Early Warning

6-48 hours of lead time based on station distance and tier
